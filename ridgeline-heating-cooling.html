The problem

A one-man shop can’t answer every call

Nick McIntier owns Ridgeline and runs the trucks himself. When he’s on a roof or under a furnace, the phone goes to voicemail — and a full-time receptionist isn’t worth it for a small shop.

In home services that’s expensive: 85% of callers who hit voicemail never call back, and 62% just dial a competitor. Every missed call is a missed job — and Nick had no way to know how many were slipping away.

What we did

Put a receptionist on the line that never misses

Step 1

Built “Riley”

We trained an AI phone receptionist on Ridgeline’s business, so it answers like a real member of the team.

Step 2

Answered every call

Riley picks up 24/7 — the calls Nick can’t get to on a roof — and collects each caller’s name, number, and what they need.

Step 3

Routed the real leads

Qualified leads get texted straight to Nick to call back; spam, misdials, and vendor calls are filtered out automatically.

The result

Every call answered, around the clock

Over 8 months, Riley answered ~1,560 calls — 195 a month on average, peaking near 230/month through the winter heating rush. At a measured 24.7% capture rate, that’s roughly 48 real leads a month — about 385 jobs that would otherwise have gone to voicemail and walked to a competitor.

When a phone-provider switch briefly cut call-forwarding to the agent, the gap was obvious enough that Ridgeline is bringing Riley back online. The calls were too valuable to lose.
