The problem

A practice owner with no time to sort AI out

Dr. Jeffrey Hammond is a podiatrist, a practice owner, a dad — and a 250-mile ultramarathoner. He knew AI could help, but he didn’t have the bandwidth to research platforms and tell real tools from hype.

Three things quietly ate his week: he finalized every chart note himself, patients only rebooked when someone remembered to ask, and his software didn’t talk to itself. He’d rather raise prices than add headcount — so anything new had to earn its keep.

The big one

Bring patients back automatically

Custom orthotics should be re-checked every 12–18 months — today that only happens when someone remembers to ask. A simple rebooking sequence reaches every patient whose window has passed. One or two rebooked patients a month covers the entire tool stack.

Overwhelming became a Monday to-do list

Dr. Hammond walked away with a prioritized, costed roadmap in hand — four wins live in week one for under $130/month, well inside his budget, plus a clear growth lever that funds the rest. No hidden fees, no pressure, built on the software he already uses.
